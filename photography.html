366 Photo a Day

I committed to creating and sharing a photo every single day for 2016. Though my quality may occasionally be inconsistent due to the other obligations that I have to balance, I use this project as an excuse to experiment more, and have a little more fun, with my photography. You can find most of my project at my Instagram (with only one missing period when I lacked access to Instagram.

People in their Places

In this ongoing project, I am photographing students and other Harvard residents where they feel most comfortable: their own rooms. I am beginning with a single dorm, with fairly consistent room shapes, in order to explore the way that people take the blank slate college dorm that they are provided and make it their temporary home.

Past

Portraits of PBPSS

PBPSS stands for PRISE, BLISS, PRIMO, SHARP, and SURGH, the five programs that make up Harvard's Summer Research Program. Each program has its own special focus, but the students in them live in the same building and eat together on weekday dinners. Furthermore, program participants are given funding to host their own community building events. As a member of PRISE, the science and engineering- focused program, I decided to produce, along with my friend Cherie, a HONY-style photo series of PBPSS members, which was posted along with a brief interview of each member. This was also the motivation for my first DSLR camera purchase, something that has majorly impacted my life ever since.
