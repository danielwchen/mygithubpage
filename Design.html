From OneWheel to Everyone Wheel

Diversifying the OneWheel Customer Base

In collaboration with Sophie Altchek
OneWheel is a new way to travel. Exciting as an extreme board sport while staying easier to learn than a bicycle, the OneWheel has the potential to appeal to a diverse group of people with an updated marketing campaign and some design changes. These recommendations were proposed to the founder of OneWheel directly.

On the Lamb - The Board Game

Learning to Design for Fun

In collaboration with Anne Cheng
What makes something fun? It is a different challenge than designing for functionality or nostalgia. In this original board game, your crafty sheep and a fierce wolf must collaborate to ensure your sheep is the last sheep standing. Anne and I aim to tickle your funny bone while testing your ability for deception.

Double Desker

Rethinking Personal Workspace, Inspired by Used Furniture

In collaboration with Katie Steinman
A trip to the Harvard Recycling Center left Kate, Ajay, and I in possession of an old adjustable swivel chair with an interesting feature. It's max height was nearly twice as high as that of a typical office chair, sparking an idea for a new conception of work spaces.

Chill Pill

A Bed for Everyone

In collaboration with Kelsey Ichikawa and Xindong Zhang
According to the National Law Center on Homelessness and Poverty, homeless populations exceeds shelter capacity in virtually all US cities. Personal interactions with the homeless people of Boston have informed me that many cannot find proper shelter, especially in the winters. This inspired me to work on Chill Pill, an adaptation of Japanese capsule hotels to an American context. This proposal, with a plan to market to travelers as well, won first prize at the Synergy pitch competition, held at the end of its week-long incubation program,
